Welcome to Sushi College Tokyo

Experience Authentic Sushi Mastery in Tokyo

Sushi College Tokyo offers an intensive one-month professional training program designed specifically for international students who dream of becoming authentic sushi chefs.

Located in the prestigious Ebisu district of Shibuya, Tokyo, our college provides hands-on training under the guidance of master chefs with decades of experience. You'll learn traditional techniques, ingredient selection, knife skills, and the cultural philosophy behind this ancient culinary art.

What You'll Learn

Course Curriculum

Week 1

Fundamentals & Knife Skills

  • Introduction to sushi history and culture
  • Knife selection, handling, and maintenance
  • Basic cutting techniques (hosogiri, sainome, tanzaku)
  • Fish anatomy and filleting basics
  • Rice preparation and seasoning
Week 2

Nigiri Mastery

  • Advanced knife techniques (sogi-giri, usu-zukuri)
  • Nigiri forming techniques
  • Fish selection and quality assessment
  • Working with various fish types
  • Proper wasabi preparation
Week 3

Rolls & Specialties

  • Maki (rolled sushi) techniques
  • Inside-out rolls (uramaki)
  • Hand rolls (temaki)
  • Gunkan and creative presentations
  • Sashimi plating and artistry
Week 4

Professional Practice

  • Speed and efficiency training
  • Menu development
  • Kitchen management and hygiene
  • Customer service at the sushi counter
  • Final examination and certification

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

I've never handled a knife before. Is that okay?

Absolutely! Over 80% of our students start with no experience and graduate at a professional level. Our teaching method combines theoretical understanding with repetitive practice, making it accessible regardless of age.

I'm worried about keeping up with the classes.

Don't worry! Our instructors are not just skilled craftsmen—they are experienced teaching professionals. During practical sessions, they provide individual guidance and work to resolve any issues, so you can learn with confidence.

Can I continue classes after taking a day off?

For short absences (1 day to a few days), we have a follow-up system using self-study time after classes. For longer absences (1 week or more), we will accommodate you by either continuing or changing your class schedule.

How much Japanese cuisine will I learn?

Our curriculum includes Japanese cuisine fundamentals such as dashi (broth) preparation, sashimi, and tempura. We also include decorative vegetable cutting techniques that go beyond typical sushi restaurant skills.

What's the difference from culinary vocational schools?

The amount of hands-on practice is overwhelmingly greater. Unlike group-based culinary schools, at Sushi College Tokyo, you master fish preparation through intensive repetition. *Note: We are not a culinary vocational school, so chef's licenses are not included.

Can you help me find accommodation?

We ask that students arrange their own accommodation. We appreciate your understanding.

Do you offer installment payment plans or loans?

Yes, installment payment through loans is available. You can split payments into 3 to 84 monthly installments, making professional sushi training more accessible.
